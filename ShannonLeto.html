SHANNON LETO

Shannon Leto is an American musician, songwriter, and entrepreneur best known as the drummer for the band Thirty Seconds to Mars, which he co-founded with his brother Jared Leto. Born on March 9, 1970, in Bossier City, Louisiana, Shannon developed a passion for music at a young age and became known for his dynamic and powerful drumming style, which incorporates elements of rock, electronic, and orchestral music.

As part of Thirty Seconds to Mars, Shannon has contributed to the band’s international success, with albums such as *A Beautiful Lie* (2005), "This Is War" (2009), and "Love, Lust, Faith and Dreams" (2013). The band is recognized for its conceptual music, cinematic videos, and record-breaking world tours. Shannon’s drumming is a key element of the band’s sound, and he is celebrated for his intense live performances.

Beyond his music career, Shannon is also involved in entrepreneurial ventures, including his coffee brand, Black Fuel Trading Company. He is known for his interest in art, photography, and environmental causes, often using his platform to advocate for sustainability and creativity.

Shannon Leto remains an influential figure in modern rock, admired for his dedication to his craft and his innovative approach to both music and business.
